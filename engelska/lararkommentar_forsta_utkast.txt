Thank you for your first draft. It is good that you have started with your analysis. However, I am afraid I cannot clearly see a strong thesis statement in your text. I think your thesis statement looks more descriptive than argumentative.

The thesis should consist of ONE clear statement that someone can disagree with. Please note that you are NOT supposed to write a book review.


Before sending the final version in, there are some adjustments you need to make / some points you need to pay attention to:

- Please note that the final version of this essay (in the last assignment) must be submitted at least two days BEFORE AM2.
- You should not refer to yourself in a formal text. Instead write in the third person. First person pronouns (I, me, our, we etc.) are too informal for academic writing. At University, professors prefer students to write in third person.
- Please strictly adhere to the structure of the 5-paragraph essay structure and the PEEL-method.
- Remember to put your thesis statement in the introduction so the reader understands from the beginning what the analysis is going to be about. *)
- Please use linking words to make the connections between your thoughts/paragraphs clearer.
- Introduce the final paragraph with a linking word, like "Finally, ...". Then, restate your thesis and mention your main points in relation to your thesis. Imagine someone just reading your final paragraph in order to understand the essay "in a nutshell". :)
- Last but not least, do not forget to give your analysis a catchy headline that mirrors your thesis statement and novel. (Your headline should look like a short version of your thesis.)


*) This is an example of what the first paragraph (the introduction) could look like in an analysis. Think of your first introductory paragraph as a "guided tour" into your essay. (The thesis is in bold italics in the original):


The Practices of Dr. Rank   (this is the headline)

In the play A Doll's House, by Henrik Ibsen, the convention of marriage is examined and questioned for its lack of honesty. The play is set in the late 1800s, which provides the backdrop for the debate about roles of people in society. Ibsen uses the minor character, Dr. Rank, to help develop the theme of conflicts within society. This, in turn, creates connections with the plot. *Dr. Rank's function in the play is to foreshadow, symbolize, and reflect upon the truth of life and society and to break down the barrier between appearance and reality.*
