# Assignment 4 - What the Teacher Is Expecting

## Quick truth first
Your teacher is not only checking your English. They are checking if you can:
1. Build a clear argument
2. Use sources correctly
3. Connect to a real local context
4. Show your own voice and reflections
5. Speak in a structured, understandable way

---

## Rubric-style checklist (what to include)

## 1) Task completion
- Pick one topic only
- Pick one English-speaking society (you chose UK)
- Keep speech around 5-6 minutes for final submission
- Submit both audio (sound only) and transcript

## 2) Structure quality
Your speech must clearly have:
- Introduction (greeting + thesis + why this matters locally)
- 3 main points
- Conclusion (thesis restated + concrete actions + strong final line)

If structure is hard to see, grade usually drops.

## 3) Argument strength
Teacher expects:
- A clear claim (thesis)
- Evidence for each main point
- Opposing view handled directly

For Topic 1, you must answer: "stereotypes are just harmless jokes" and explain why that is wrong.

## 4) Sources and referencing
You need:
- 2 sources from Lesson 13/14
- 1 English-language source about your chosen society

What teachers often want:
- Mention source ideas naturally in speech
- Keep a short reference list in transcript
- Avoid copying long lines word-for-word

## 5) Local adaptation (very important)
Teacher is expecting UK context in:
- examples (schools, workplaces, public spaces)
- wording (community leaders, councils, local residents)
- practical actions relevant to local life

## 6) Personal voice
You should include:
- one or two personal observations
- natural human English (not robotic or too advanced)
- respectful but confident tone

This is usually what makes the work feel authentic.

## 7) Delivery (audio)
Teacher listens for:
- clear pronunciation and pace
- logical pauses between points
- confidence and professionalism

Not perfect accent. Clear communication matters more.
