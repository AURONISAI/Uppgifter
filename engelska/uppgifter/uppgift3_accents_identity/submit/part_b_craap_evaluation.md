Part B: Source Critical Evaluation (CRAAP Method)

Third source: Horizon Magazine — article on accent bias research featuring
psycholinguist Dr Alice Foucart, published by the European Commission's
research and innovation magazine.

---

**Currency**

The Horizon Magazine article is relatively recent, published as part of the
European Commission's ongoing coverage of social science research. The topic
of accent bias is very much a current issue, especialy in Europe where over
250 million people live outside their home country. Because migration and
multilingualism are still growing, a recent source is much more relevent
then an older one would be. Research on how brains process foreign accents
is also a fairly new field, so newer publications are generaly more accurate
then older ones on this specific topic.

**Relevance**

This source was directly usefull for Point 2 of my speech, where I talked
about how accent bias has real consequences. I used the research finding
that "listening to a foreign accent requires more cognitive effort" which
can lead to negative judgements about the speaker. I also used the Flemish
experiment where native speakers were rated as more intelligent and
succesful than foreign speakers. These facts gave my argument real evidence
instead of just opinons, which made the second point much stronger.

**Authority**

The source features Dr Alice Foucart, who is described as a psycholinguist —
so she has a specific academic background in how language and the brain
interact. Horizon Magazine is published by the European Commission, which
is a major and well-established institution. This makes the publisher very
credible. Its not a random blog or opinionated website, its an official
EU research publication, so I trust the editorial standards are high.

**Accuracy**

Dr Foucart's claims are backed by actual experiments, like the study with
Flemish listeners rating native vs foreign speakers. This is empirical
research not just theory. I cross-checked the general finding — that accents
affect how people judge intelligence — with the British Council article
which also touched on the same subject, so the two sources supported
each other on this point.

**Purpose**

Horizon Magazine exists to communicate European research to the general
public and policy makers. The intended audience is educated readers who
are interested in science and social issues, not just specialists.
Because its funded by the EU, there could be a slight bias toward
promoting pro-European and pro-inclusion narratives. But for my speech
this actually fits — the source's angle supports my argument that
we should address accent bias in institutions like the European Parliament.
I am aware of this bias and tried to present the information fairly.
